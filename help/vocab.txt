<h3>Reviewing Vocabulary Words</h3>
<p spacebelow=10>First click on the Vocabulary List you wish to study.  This takes you to the Vocabulary Groups screen for that list.  Here you choose which subsets of vocabulary words you wish to study from entire list.  In the image below, we clicked on the first Vocabulary List, "All Words by Frequency".  You can see the Vocabulary List name at the top of this screen.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/vocab-childlist-empty.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>By default, none of the vocabulary groups are enabled for review.  Clicking on the "Study These Words" button for any group will toggle whether you wish to include that group when you review this vocabulary list.</p>
<p spacebelow=10>In the following image, the groups "Words appearing 400 or more times" and "Words appearing 200 to 399 times" are the only groups enabled.  With both these groups enabled, you will be reviewing all words in the Greek text which appear 200 or more times.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/vocablist.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>You can enable and disable the groups in order to review the collections of vocabulary words which you are interested in.  Once you have enabled any groups to review, the "Study" link near the top of the screen will become enabled.  Clicking on that link will take you to the Vocabulary Review screen.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/vocabstudy.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>In the Vocabulary Review screen, all the words in all the groups which you selected to review are randomized and presented to you one at a time.  At the bottom of the screen, the number of words remaining to review is on the left in red and the number of words which you have seen is in blue on the right.</p>
<p spacebelow=10>After looking at the word and (hopefully) remembering its meaning, click anywhere on the screen to display the gloss and see if you correctly knew the word.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/vocabstudy-show.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>The gloss and the number of occurrences in the Greek text are shown as well as the principle parts (of verbs) and any notes which you have attached to this word.</p>
<p spacebelow=10>At the bottom there will now be four colored buttons from which you need to choose one to tell the app when you wish to next see this word in your studies.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/vocabbuttons.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>If you choose the red "Again" button, then the word will be added (randomly) to the end of your review list, and you will see it again during this review session.  If you choose any of the three buttons marked with days, however, then you will not see the word again for that many days.</p>
<p spacebelow=10>So if you choose "1 Day", then you will not see this word again in this session.  But if you review these words again tomorrow, you will again see this word.  Likewise, if you choose the "2 Days" button, then you will not see this word again while reviewing today, nor will you see it if you review these words tomorrow.  But you will see it when you review these words at least two days from now.</p>
<p spacebelow=10>The next time you see this word, your choices for how long to delay before seeing the word again will also change.  You will always have the red "Again" button which shows you the word again that same review session.  But for any other time delay you choose greater than 1 day, the next time you see the word, your previous choice will be in the green button, with half that delay to the left of it in the yellow button and twice that delay in the blue button to the right of it.</p>
<p spacebelow=10>So imagine that you choose the "4 Days" button in the above example.  Then the next time that you see the word (four days from now), you will have the following button choices:</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/vocabstudy-4.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>"4 Days" (your previous choice) is in the green button, "2 Days" (half of 4) is in the yellow button, and "8 Days" (twice 4) is in the blue button.  If you were to choose "4 Days" again, then you will see this same configuration of buttons when next you see this word.  If you were to choose "2 Days", however, then the next time you see the word (two days from now) you will see the same buttons you saw before - "1 Day", "2 Days", and "4 Days".  If you were to choose "8 Days", on the other hand, then the next time you see the word (in 8 days) you will see the following buttons.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/vocabstudy-8.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>The idea is that as you come to know words better and better, you need to review them less frequently.  So you tell the app to delay showing them to you again until further in the future.  Words you are not as familiar with, you can tell the app to show to you repeatedly, day after day, until you feel confident enough to push it out two days, then four days, etc.  Hopefully this maximizes the benefit of your vocabulary study time.</p>
<p spacebelow=10>In the upper-left of the screen, is a blue (triangle) Back icon.  Clicking that icon will take you back to the previous word so you can change the delay interval.  Your choice now will overwrite your previous choice.  You can go back as many words as you wish in this session, all the way back to the first word.  But you will need to choose a delay again for each word; there's no way to "jump" back to where you were in the list.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/vocabstudy-back.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>You can click away from the vocabulary tutor at any time using the navigation bar at the bottom of the screen.  The program will pause your session and remember where you are.  Clicking on the Vocabulary icon later will bring you back to where you left off.  You end a session by clicking on the "Exit" link at the top of the screen which will return you to the screen where you choose which vocabulary groups to review.</p>

<h3>Adding Special Entries</h3>
<p spacebelow=10>The import field also has one other "special" ability.  It allows you to create vocabulary entries which do not match dictionary words.  Right now, we have only added dictionary words, and the program automatically fills in the definition, word count, etc.  But many vocabulary lists want you to study irregular forms.  So there will be an entry for "·º¶ŒªŒ∏ŒøŒΩ" which is the aorist of "·ºîœÅœáŒøŒºŒ±Œπ".  In order to enter a form which is not a dictionary word, you use a special syntax in the import field.</p>
<p spacebelow=10>You need to make three separate entries, each separated by "::".  The first entry is the dictionary word you want to associate this entry with.  The second is the entry you wish to create, and the third is the "definition" you want associated with this entry.  So in our example above, to create an entry for "·º¶ŒªŒ∏ŒøŒΩ" we would enter:</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/vocab-childlist-import-special.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>When you click the "Add Words" link, you will see the entry in the grid does not have the dictionary entry for "·ºîœÅœáŒøŒºŒ±Œπ" but rather the "entry" and "definition" which you created separated by "::".  When you click the "Save" link, this will create the entry "·º¶ŒªŒ∏ŒøŒΩ" with the definition "aorist of ·ºîœÅœáŒøŒºŒ±Œπ".</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/vocab-childlist-special-added.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>The dictionary word (the first entry) does not need to contain diacritcs since the program will search for it with or without them.  The two other entries can be anything you want, so make sure that they appear the way you want.  The program does no checking on entries you create yourself.</p>
<p spacebelow=10>In order to type this line, you may wish to include diacritics in your Greek word, and you may want to type in English to add descriptions.  You can add diacritics to vowels using special "control characters", "[ ( ] ) / 1 \ 2 = 3". <i>(Remember that pressing the dollar sign ("$") will bring up the keyboard map which includes these diacritics.)</i></p>
<p spacebelow=10>You can type breathings, "[" or "(" for rough and "]" or ")" for smooth, and accents, "/" or "1" for accute, "\" or "2" for grave, and "=" or "3" for circumflex in front of any vowel.  So if you want an omega with a smooth breathing and a circumflex accent, you would type ")=w" or "]=w".  The order of the diacritcs does not matter.  You could type "=]w" and get the same result.  All that matters is that the verb be typed last.  Nothing will appear until after you type the vowel at which point the character "<b>·Ω¶</b>" will appear.  The control characters do not appear when you type.  If you type anything other than a vowel immediately after these characters, then they will silently discarded.  If you want to type any of the control characters and have it appear, type a space (" ") immediately after the character.  So "( " will type "(", "3 " will type "3", etc.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><i>(Please note that the app does not check that what you typed makes sense in Greek.  You can type breathings on characters at any place in a word, you can put an accent on every vowel in a word, etc.  The keyboard allows you to type whatever you want in Greek; it doesn't check what you type.)</i></p>
<p spacebelow=10>You can also switch your keybaord back to type in English (or whatever your native keyboard language is) by pressing the ampersand key ("&").  <i>(Desktop versions can also press the Tab key.)</i>  Pressing the "&" key repeatedly toggles the keyboard back and forth between Greek and English.  The keyboard language will be displayed in the watermark of the import field, "Gr" for Greek and "En" for English.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/vocab-childlist-import-english.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>You can cancel the creation of a new group at any time by clicking the "back" arrow in the upper-left.  This will return you to the vocabulary groups screen.  Any changes you have made in the groups creator will be forgotten.  The only exception is the import field.  While canceling the creation of a new group will remove any words you have added to that list, anything you have typed into the import field will still be there next time you create a new list.</p>

<h3>A Note About Warnings</h3>
<p spacebelow=10>If you do a lot of work creating and/or deleting vocabulary lists and groups, then you are going to see a lot of warnings.  Every time you delete anything, the progam will ask you for confirmation because deletions are permenant.  But if you are deleting a lot, this can get tiresome.  You can turn the delete warnings off by going to the Search Page, changing the Search drop-down to "English Definitions" and typing "warnings:delete:off" into the search field.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/warnings-delete-off.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>Now the program will never ask for confirmation for a vocabulary deletion.  Press "Delete" and it's gone.  This change remains the default every time you run the program.  If you want to turn the warnings back on, you must do so manually by typing "warnings:delete:on" into that same field.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/warnings-delete-on.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>In addition, if you create a number of vocabulary groups by typing or pasting words in directly, every time you click the "Add Words" link, if there are any words in the import field which the app cannot identify, then you will receive a warning and those words will remain in the import field.  You can also turn this warning off by going to the Search Page, changing the Search drop-down to "English Definitions" and typing "warnings:import:off" into the search field.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/warnings-import-off.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>As with turning off the delete warnings, this change remains the default every time you run the program.  If you want to turn the warnings back on, you must do so manually by typing "warnings:import:on" into that same field.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/warnings-import-on.png"></p>
