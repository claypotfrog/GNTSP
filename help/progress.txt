<p spacebelow=10>You can manually track your progress in reading through the Greek text if you so desire.  While on the Read page, click on the blue ribbon icon at the top of the screen to the left of the name of the book you are reading.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/bookmark_icon.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>This brings up a page that shows you which verses (if any) you have bookmarked or highlighted, as well as your reading progress.  The selection chooser at the top shows you which you are viewing.  We are interested in tracking our progress, so click the word "Progress" in the selection chooser.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/progress_screen.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>This brings up a listing of all the books in the Greek New Testament.  The top line shows you how many chapters out of the entire Greek New Testament (which contains 260 chapters in total) you have marked as read.  After that, each line shows you a book and how many chapters in that book you have marked as read out of the total number of chapters in that book.  Since we are just starting out, nothing is marked, so all the books say "0/X". (<i>If you have marked any chapters as read, then the total will be in bold.</i>)</p>
<p spacebelow=10>At first, all the books are collapsed, so you cannot see any of the chapters to mark them as read.  To open a book, click on the book name or the blue caret on the left side of the line.  This will expand the entry so you can see (and mark) chapters.  Let's assume that we are reading the gospel of John, so we'll click on the line for "John".</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/john_expanded.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>Now the blue caret on the left points down (indicating that the book is expanded) and we see a line for every chapter in John.  Click on the text "chapter X" or the switch on the right side of the line to "flip" the switch and indicate that we have read this book.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/john_expanded_marked.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>Here we have clicked on chapters 1 through 3 to show that we have read those three chapters in the Greek.  The switch on each line is now highlighted in blue and on the "John" line, it now shows "3/21" which means we have read three of the twenty-one chapters in the gospel of John.  In addition, the top line also shows that we have read three of the 260 chapters in the entire New Testament.</p>
<p spacebelow=10>You can click on the book name or the blue caret to collapse any line that expanded.  In the image below, we have clicked on the "John" line again to collapse it.  We cannot see the individual chapters, but we still see that we have marked three of them as read.</p>
<p spacebelow=10><img src="binfile:help/images/john_collapsed.png"></p>
<p spacebelow=10>This feature is entirely manual.  The program has no way of knowing what you actually read, so you must flip these switches yourself for each chapter after you have read it.  But hopefully having an easy way to track your reading progress will encourage you as you read through the Greek New Testament.</p>
